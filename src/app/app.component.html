Guiding Principles


Lean Concept

Eliminate waste as much as possible. Evaluate waste especially in processes, software, and notification/alerts. Do not implement solutions that is going to be a maintaining nightmare. Take actions only when you are ready.

Understand Changes

People, Process, Technology. People always come first. If you want to ensure a successful change, always make sure your people are on board. Share your vision and the purpose of the change. Assure people following you that changes are not scary and will lead to a better future. Understand what you are trying to get to up front and fine tune your process based on your data.

Innovation

Technologies change rapidly. Open yourself to adopt new thinking and new way of doing things and focus on the problems your customers are facing days in and days out. Fail fast and move on. Help your customers understand zero disruption is not a good thing and manage their expectations as they are not as tech savvy.

People

Happy people will result in happy customers. Every people is different so you need to handle them differently. Don't manage your people as if only their outputs matter. Show empathy. Be fair and be transparent as much as possible. Spend more time observe and listen to their needs. Make them feel safe to talk to you about anything. Challenge, trust and encourage them to go out of their comfort zone. Remind yourself to praise and celebrate successes often. Ensure the right people is in the right job.

User Experience

Everything should just work. Focus on making things simple and responsive. Customer's requirements are important but do not let your customers dictate your user interface. Leverage this as an opportunity to dazzle your customers.

Motivation/Retention

The three main components to motivate employees to increase their performance and satisfaction are Autonomy (our desire to be self directed), Mastery (urge to get better skills), and Purpose (desire to do something that has meaning and important).
